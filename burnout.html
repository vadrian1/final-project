Creative Burnout

Adrian Vergara, April 15th 2024

Everyone has experienced burnout. Whether it was work, school, or something similar, burnout can happen in any aspect of life. The official definition of burnout is: to cause to fail, wear out, or become exhausted especially from overwork or overuse. And it's not something that is isolated to one aspect of your life; burnout in school for example can cause you to be exhausted in your social life, and negatively impact your life overall.


Of course I have expereienced burnout with school like many others, but where I find burnout incredibly frusturating is creative burnout. I started to produce music as a hobby in sophomore year of highschool. Using a pirated version of FL Studio, I had fun creating silly beats and tracks and actually created things I thought that didn't sound half bad. As I created more, I found the process ever more freeing and relaxing as a creative outlet. As someone who used to be a visual artist but has created less over the years, it was amazing.


As time went on, I found myself improving. I began to want to take this hobby of mine more seriously. I wanted to create things that were better and start sharing my music. But as I transitioned into taking music more seriously, I found that it wasn't as fun as it used to be. The things that I created, I became less happy with. I became overcritical over my work, which led to me being more frusturated, which led to me wanting to create less. Eventually I thought everything I made was bad, so I stopped.
